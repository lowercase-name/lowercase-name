yes, it's a letter.

no, really. not, like, "this is my cute nickname", not "an alternative to a legal name that sounds less fake", it is actually what is on my birth certificate.

it is not short for anything. it is not an initial. there is no "additional" first name. it is my name, and it precisely one letter long. yes, i routinely ruin the lives of I.T. workers and office secretaries everywhere, every time my codified legal name has to rub up against their 2-character-minimum input forms. i have, more than once, had database listings, and at one point, a package, under the name "the letter j".

why?

well, why are you named who you are?

i think, especially within certain groups of identities, it's easy to get caught up in thinking any of this matters. like, if you don't ascribe a sufficient enough "meaning" to a name, it somehow doesn't count, like someone's going to call you out in public for not knowing a sufficient amount about the origins of delilah or elinor or whatever.

it's the mentality that spawns kids named *prophecy*, where you clearly picked it because it both sounds unique and it's a word you know the meaning to, and you're probably setting up the poor kid for a childhood of nothing but bullying, and when they finally gain independence, at age 18 and off to college when you aren't looking, they might not even need a lifetime of gender confusion to be convinced to pony up the name change fee.

but at least it had *meaning*. even if it meant nothing to them.

it's an arbitrary problem to have, and most people know it. you can walk up to any given man on planet earth and ask "hey why are you chris" and he would just shrug, because, uh, he just is? his mom picked it, he thinks. maybe his dad actually? was it rude to assume it was his mom, who knows, it's not like he asked either of them, they probably just picked something safe and he thinks it's pretty alright. he's chris because that's his name, man, what are you expecting?

chris has a point, i think. it's only your name when you hear it, and you definitely know it's yours. when i hear my name, it's what i know is mine. what other reason do i need?
