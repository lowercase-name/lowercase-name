let me tell you about the dumbest apartment window i've ever had to use.

imagine you're in a condo, downtown vancouver, british columbia canada. the rent is incredibly cheap by this city's standards, for reasons you will not understand at first. it's about to become summer, so it's a bit warm out, and your windows are all floor-to-ceiling, uninsulated windows pointed directly at the afternoon sun, which makes it even warmer. only one of these windows can be opened, the one at the center, and it is *gigantic*.

<as-dithered-image src="/assets/condo-design/window1.jpg" alt="a reenactment of a condo window setup, the openable window being 10 feet by 2 feet" cutoff="0.25"></as-dithered-image>

it has a handle, which you turn, and after you turn on the handle, you can then pull to open it.

when you pull the handle, to this door-like window, to open the window, how do you think it opens?

go on. honest guesses only.

...

...

anyways, the correct answer is that <b>***it tilts vertically***</b>

<as-dithered-image src="/assets/condo-design/window2.jpg" alt="that same window is now being tilted forward, from the top; the diagram also doesn't get it" cutoff="0.25"></as-dithered-image>

this is the tilt state! because this ten foot tall window is a tilt casement window.

except, actually, no it's not, because that's not how it looks fully opened! this is an awkward middle state the window forces you into for its actual open state.

which is when you pull the entire window out. the whole thing. no hinges.

<as-dithered-image src="/assets/condo-design/window3.jpg" alt="there is now just a window hanging in the air i swear to god this is a real window i lived with" cutoff="0.25"></as-dithered-image>

it is hanging from the ceiling. you now have the world's worst curtain.

that's step 2. if you then pulled it to the side, you get to your final state, a gigantic open space, mostly covered by a handrail. now you have a porch?

<as-dithered-image src="/assets/condo-design/window4.jpg" alt="the window-curtain is now moved to the side; the diagram says 'why', which i also say" cutoff="0.25"></as-dithered-image>

except, to be clear, those rails go right up against the window. your apartment is one entire porch, i guess.

i cannot give you a name for this style of window, because i doubt there exists enough of these windows to necessitate one. it was custom made, for this specific building, and i would be shocked if anyone was ever insane enough to order it for a second one.

it's the worst window on planet earth. it is a window that cannot be merely *slightly* open. if you want any fresh air, you're opening up all twenty of god's own square footage of window to the outside world, bugs and downtown smog included.

this sucks! this sucks.

but, whatever. it's fine that your only window is a monstrosity of engineering vaguely shaped like a normal window. if you lived here like me, you probably lived in worse apartments! it's fine. it's vancouver, the weather here is always barely warm anyways, and it's june 2021 and you're right downtown where everything else is, so you're about to have the best summer of your

you know. i've lived in some real bad apartments. illegal duplexes, literally-rotting slumhouses, suspicious buildings where everyone tells you not to drink the water because the pipes are all rusted.

those were bad. *they weren't designed to be bad.* there's something uniquely stupid about toasting in a 45-degree celsius humidex, with no way to install air conditioning, because someone tried to be too clever about engineering *a window*.

why would anyone choose to design a window like that?

well, why would you design a condo like condos are designed?

---

a very long time ago, housing stopped being built for the people living in them. 

they became for investors. they might be buying it after it's built, or ahead of time, to fund its construction, but the one thing that ties them together is how they'll never step foot in a single one of the "speculative properties" they're paying for.

these are investors who simply need to believe that, even if they won't live in them, *someone would want to.* 

but they are also rich. they wouldn't know what a normal human being might want in a home; they only know spaces large and hyperspecific, and they don't have to bother with any of the inconveniences or cleanup involved with their flaws.

all they can see are photos, so the photos must be tuned perfectly to their desires.

everything must be sleek. there has to be a *view*. the kitchens need to look like marble, and the floors need to look like tiles, and you *have* to be able to imagine yourself leaning against a rail, on a porch --- or something like a porch --- as if you're the special boy in a movie all about how cool you are.

whether or not any of it works is optional.

<as-dithered-image src="/assets/condo-design/oven.jpg" alt="a sink with the faucet right in there and the water leaking; an intercom with a handwritten note reading DON'T WORK, CALL XX-XXXX; a gas stove setting a fire alarm off"></as-dithered-image>

the sinks can be shallow, and leak. the "security" can break, constantly, leaving others stranded out of their own homes. ventilation from the very-instagrammable gas stoves can be non-existant, so long as it just looks like there's an oven fan that's supposed to work.

why would the investor care? they don't experience the sink. they can't smell the smoke. all they see is what they see, in pictures.

<as-dithered-image src="/assets/condo-design/tub1.jpg" alt="a cruddy little rectangular bathtub"></as-dithered-image>

that window is the funniest example, but i find that it's always the bathroom where this is most obvious. a good bathroom is, while sometimes homely, mostly ugly; the toilet is the boring shape human bodies need it to be, a good bathtub is a weird ridged shape, and a good showerhead comes with a long, extendable hose, because it's the one part of your house where you could not care about aesthetics before function.

but the condo bathroom is never like that. most of the time, there's no tub at all --- the cool guys in the movies all stand, of course --- but even when there is, they're designed like someone was ashamed of a tub being there, so minimalist that it's practically worthless. the showerhead is unmoving, usually not even adjustable, and god forbid you want to change it, because it's almost definitely a permanent fixture.

but some of them go one step further, with the curtain.

or lack, thereof.

<as-dithered-image src="/assets/condo-design/tub2.jpg" alt="that tub, but now there's a stupid bit of glass poking out in place of a curtain"></as-dithered-image>

in its place, they will bestow upon you one, singular, unmoving glass panel, covering only half the tub, and being permanently glued to the wall. usually, the walls would literally stick out, around the panel, making absolutely sure that you couldn't even put up a curtain rod if you wanted to.

when you shower, do you keep your shower curtain halfway open? do you think there's a good, efficient idea in doing that?

<as-dithered-image src="/assets/condo-design/tub3.jpg" alt="that tub, but now the water's leaking outside"></as-dithered-image>

of course you don't.

it's so, very obviously stupid to do it like this. it's *so* obviously stupid, the bathroom walls had to be designed so you wouldn't immediately attempt to fix the mistake they made.

but it sure looks pretty, doesn't it? doesn't it look *sleek*, when it's just one solid piece of glass?

i'm sure the millionaire who bought that condo to rent out thought it was very lovely.

---

this is, to some small extent, an excuse to complain about bad housing.

it's also a complaint about everything.

writers have to explicitly write [on the assumption their audiences aren't paying attention,](https://screenrant.com/netflix-movies-repeat-plot-viewers-attention-span-phones/) repeating plot points over and over so that people *who choose not to engage with their work* don't get confused.

big-budget live service games designed to become the next fortnite [die out quickly,](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-02-26/the-story-behind-the-failure-of-highguard) as multi-billion dollar funders fail to learn that audiences might want more than two or three genres on repeat. the few times such live service games succeed, their updates delete aspects of gameplay, [sometimes eating itself alive in deleting so much,](https://www.pcgamer.com/games/fps/with-steam-launch-concurrents-at-an-all-time-low-for-destiny-2s-latest-expansion-i-think-bungie-is-finally-paying-the-price-for-years-of-mistakes-the-biggest-being-the-time-it-removed-paid-content/) as if everyone in charge resents the possibility someone might want to play it.

a.i. --- that annoying little topic again --- is everywhere, [despite the average person deeply loathing it.](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/891724/nbc-news-march-2026-poll-ai-ice) it's forced into every program you need to use and every website you have no choice but to continue being on, and the ones implementing it are too busy looking excitedly at the investors who need a.i. in everything to realize how they're alienating their own base. developers [who don't stand to gain any productivity from a.i.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.20245) are stuck having to pretend it does, as they work two or three jobs at once, because their boss thought it could replace their coworkers.

these hallucinations of "what the average person *truly* wants" are all pushed, intensely, by superiors who do not understand the average person, because it would require them to be like anyone normal. they cannot understand; their lives of decadence are simply too far removed from the rest of the world to understand. they remain unpunished, because nobody has a choice but to humor them.

<b>**this is the condo trap.**</b> a world not designed for anyone who might have to spend time existing in it, but designed for what someone else, far removed from the troubles and comforts you or i might experience, thinks the rest of us might want.

it was already enough of a mistake to let this happen to the places we live in. why are we letting it dictate how we create?
